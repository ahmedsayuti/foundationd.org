Strengthening Vaccine Awareness in North Dakota: A Community Approach to Protecting Vulnerable Populations

By Margaret L. Thornton  |  October 14, 2024

Community health leaders and caregivers gathered at the statewide vaccine awareness event hosted by the Foundation for a Healthy North Dakota.

The Foundation for a Healthy North Dakota, in collaboration with the Grandparents for Vaccines Coalition, is pleased to host the statewide health education event focused on strengthening vaccine awareness and protecting vulnerable populations from preventable diseases. This program builds on the Foundation’s mission to empower communities with reliable health information and address critical challenges such as low vaccination coverage, where currently 1 in 5 children in North Dakota are not fully protected against measles, mumps, and rubella. The event brought together community leaders, parents, and caregivers particularly grandparents who play a vital role in family health decisions—to engage in evidence-based discussions on vaccine-preventable disease trends and practical strategies to improve immunization uptake across the state.

We are grateful to Dr. Iyobosa Sonia Omoregie our featured expert speaker on this topic. Dr. Omoregie is a Post-Graduate Research Fellow at the Center for Social Research (CSR) at North Dakota State University (NDSU), with a Master’s degree in Public Health specializing in Epidemiology and a Medical Degree from Kyiv Medical University, Ukraine. Her work has been instrumental in developing the MyDakota Health , where she pioneered surveillance methodologies, risk-transmission models, and county-level disease analytics that provide real-time insights into vaccine-preventable disease patterns. During the session, she presented data-driven trends translating complex epidemiological findings into actionable guidance for families and community stakeholders. Attendees gained critical insights into how declining vaccination rates correlate with outbreak risks and learned practical steps to safeguard children and communities across North Dakota.
